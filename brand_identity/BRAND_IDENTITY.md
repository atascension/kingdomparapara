# Brand Identity — Kingdom Para Para

*Prepared by Marie Enro // July 2025*

---

## Brand Overview

Kingdom Para Para is a Spirit-led storehouse for the Body of Christ, offering devotionals, study tools, and inner healing resources to help believers walk in intimacy with Jesus and fullness of purpose. Grounded in Scripture, this business equips the Bride of Christ to prosper in soul and life while preparing for His return. The visual identity is bold and welcoming, focusing on abundance in Christ, with a theme of being set apart for His glory. Through healing and cleansing, God is able to use His children to further the impact of His Kingdom.

> "Therefore if anyone cleanses himself from the latter, he will be a vessel for honor, sanctified and useful for the Master, prepared for every good work."
> — II Timothy 2:21 NKJV

## The Logo Suite

The primary logo is both modern and minimal, with a subtly designed treasure chest with two 'P's inside. The two 'P's represent the doubling of the word "para," in addition to being symbolic of two people sat on either side, as they will be when consulting services begin to take place.

## Image Direction

The image direction for Kingdom Para Para takes on three themes that all point to abundance in Christ — **'Set Apart,' 'Planted by the Rivers'** and **'The Riches.'**

Depending on the type of resource being designed, different themes can be used to accompany a particular message, whichever seems fitting; however the image style should remain cohesive. The imagery used should reflect stillness, peace and abundance.

Throughout the imagery, vessels are used to signify that God's children can be used, "for honor, sanctified and useful for the Master." (II Timothy 2:21) Other imagery includes grain, which leans into the story of Joseph, collecting immeasurable grain ahead of the famine — this ties into the founder's story being connected to the life of Joseph, as she builds a storehouse for the Kingdom.

Other imagery reflects being planted by the rivers (Psalm 1 / Jeremiah 17), green pastures (Psalm 23) and the cow which plays a significant part in the Kingdom Para Para story, representing abundance and provision.

The last theme — The Riches — incorporates imagery of treasure chests and precious jewels symbolising the riches stored in a believer. The heart of a person is like a treasure chest, so it's important for that to be displayed purposefully throughout the visual identity.

### The Three Themes

#### Set Apart
Imagery reflecting stillness, peace and abundance. Vessels are a central motif, signifying that God's children can be sanctified and useful for the Master (II Timothy 2:21).

#### Planted by the Rivers
Imagery rooted in Psalm 1, Jeremiah 17, and Psalm 23. Includes grain (referencing the story of Joseph building a storehouse ahead of famine) and cows (representing abundance and provision).

#### The Riches
Imagery featuring treasure chests and precious jewels, symbolising the riches stored in a believer. The heart of a person is like a treasure chest and should be displayed purposefully throughout the visual identity.
